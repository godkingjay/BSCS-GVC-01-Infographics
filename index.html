About AI

Artificial intelligence (AI) is the replication or approximation of human intelligence in machines. The ideal feature of artificial intelligence is its ability to rationalize and execute actions that have the best likelihood of reaching a certain goal. The goals of artificial intelligence include computer-enhanced learning, thinking, and perception.

AI in a Global Scale

AI will help numerous sectors and industries streamline and improve their processes. AI is not a far-off vision, but rather something that is now being integrated and used in a range of industries. Agriculture, Economics, Education, Healthcare, and Business are examples of such fields. There are countless examples of AI already having an impact on the world and significantly enhancing human capabilities.

Agriculture

  • A. Intelligent Spraying of Chemicals
    • AI technology aids in the detection of plant diseases, pests, and farm nutrition deficiencies. AI sensors can detect and target weeds before determining which herbicide to use in the area.
  • AI-Based Robots for Farm Harvesting
    • AI-based robotic machines can perform bulk harvesting with greater accuracy and speed. These machines aid in increasing productivity and reducing waste from crops left in the field.
  • Crop Yield Predictions and Price Forecasts
    • AI can assist in pest and disease detection, estimating tomato output and yield, and forecasting prices. They can advise farmers and governments on future price patterns, demand levels, crop types to sow for best benefit, pesticide use, and so on.

Economics

  • AI-Powered Decision-Making
    • AI will enable government authorities to forgo outdated data collection methods in favor of data-driven judgments. Data-driven monitoring, policy evaluation, and analysis will become possible thanks to advances in information technology.
  • Labor Automation
    • AI can automate non-routine, cognitive tasks performed by highly skilled professionals, freeing them up to work on more essential tasks.
